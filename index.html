About

In a fast-evolving software landscape, these four guiding principles can serve as a reliable north star:

  1. Do what's best for the user: When we find ourselves deep in a problem, always take a step back and reflect. The solution that matters is the one that increases the livelihood, user experience, utility and satisfaction of the end user.
  2. Simplicity and Usability: Engineers love to solve problems, which often means we invent unnecessary problems to be solved. Automate/abstract when it will lead to a better product or service; but Complexity for the sake of complexity is always a bad idea.
  3. Ethical Data is the Shovel of AI: AI's potential depends on the data that fuels it, and users don't appreciate implicit data collection. Make responsible tools that make data and trends so obvious, you'd have to struggle to find hidden data collection services.
  4. The Value of Skilled Engineers: Systems like Agile or Scrum provide structure, but ultimately, it's dedicated, skilled engineers who drive success. Be principled in your software decisions, and be humble to others. Maximize your and others' skills while minimizing ego.
